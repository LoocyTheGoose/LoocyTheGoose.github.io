Hollow

The year of 1969...

During 1969, the world found itself in a very interesting place. With the Cold War sparking a space race, America found itself landing on the moon. It was an extraordinary time for America, the first successful moon landing bringing samples and glory back to Earth with much fanfare.

Among conspiracy theorists however, a group of people started prancing the country in protest, seemingly disturbed by what was considered to be the dawn of the end of times. These people were brushed off by the general public and eventually forgotten by the American populace. With America celebrating such a success, people started looking forward to the turn of the millenia, expecting many a technological advancement from such a successful mission.


The Year is 1999...

Throughout the end of '98 and through the beginning of '99, news has spread of a viral contagion making people irritable and aggressive. The West of the United States has seen large amounts of devastation across state borders. The East is now experiencing something similar...

As more and more people find themselves falling ill, more and more destruction plagues the land. The county of Hairburg, somewhere on the coast of Maryland is one of the first of the Eastern lands to fall victim to near total collapse. With government facilities running out of fumes, entire cities find themselves devastated. In a last ditch effort, the PTA, a secretive government agency is activated. As panic set in, skilled individuals who were formerly part of the general public turn from their duties of protecting those they love, moving out of their previously safe establishments and leaving friends and family behind. Gathering themselves, they founded the structure of the Goreville Quarantine zone and began leading civilians to one of the few remaining safe places within it.

While this has worked, is it true safety? Or is this indicative of something more foreboding...

Known Information...

Experiencing the turmoil of the outbreak, the living - the most affected - have seen countless horrors before their eyes. Those who were once loved now turned to violence and hunger, their bodies appearing to rapidly decay without seeming to die. Some survivors appear to have a form of resistance to the virus, dubbed "X-39". Where most who are infected present with necrotic decay soon after infection, a rare genetic resistance in these survivors seems to push the virus back, staving those who would otherwise die, off, from the horrific death and subsequent reanimation.

Two Types of Resistance


Type 1 Immunity

Those who happen to be type 1 immune are capable of pushing back the virus after a rigorous fever. While they are still susceptible to the airborne strain of the virus, it will only make them incredibly ill after a period of exposure.
Those with type 1 immunity must keep their faces covered with a particulate filter in order to prevent too much exposure. If infected, these individuals will experience a fever capable of killing them if not treated properly. During this fever, they may experience an allure to the virus, which is often the killer of infected immune persons. The sense of a presence beyond them seems to draw them toward other infected.


Type 2 Immunity

Those with Type 2 immunity are resistant to the airborne strain, only finding threat from the saliva of the infected. Much like Type 1 immunity, if infected, these individuals will come down with a major fever capable of killing them, experiencing much of the same symptoms as those with Type 1 immunity. They will find themselves wanting to walk into hordes, feeling as if the only place they will find home is in the infection.
While Type 2 immunity prevents susceptibility to the airborne strain, those with this immunity must still take caution not to come into contact with the saliva of the infected.

The Infection


The first signs...

  • Irritability
    • People become irrationally angry at the slightest provocation - often yelling and becoming physically violent.
  • Increased Hunger
    • Those infected find themselves craving more and more food - specifically meat
  • Headache
  • Excessive Sweating
  • Lethargy

Other signs...

  • Necrosis
  • Blindness
  • Loss of motor function
  • Loss of speech
  • Loss of hand-eye motor coordination
  • Sustained daze
  • Black spider-like rotting under the skin

Final stages...

  • Ragged breathing
  • Inability to communicate at all
  • Near total Necrosis of the entire body
  • Death
  • Post-mortem activity
    • Continued movement after death
    • Lingering instincts such as hunger and aggression
    • Walking
    • Attempts at vocalisation
