Hollow

The Year is 1999...

Throughout the end of '98 and through the beginning of '99, news has spread of a viral contagion making people irritable and aggressive. The West of the United States has seen large amounts of devastation across state borders. The East is now experiencing something similar...

As more and more people find themselves falling ill, more and more destruction plagues the land. The county of Hairburg, somewhere on the coast of Maryland is one of the first of the Eastern lands to fall victim to near total collapse. With government facilities running out of fumes, entire cities find themselves devastated. In a last ditch effort, the PTA, a secretive government agency is activated. As panic set in, skilled individuals who were formerly part of the general public turn from their duties of protecting those they love, moving out of their previously safe establishments and leaving friends and family behind. Gathering themselves, they founded the structure of the Goreville Quarantine zone and began leading civilians to one of the few remaining safe places within it.

While this has worked, is it true safety? Or is this indicative of something more foreboding...

Known Information...

Experiencing the turmoil of the outbreak, the living - the most affected - have seen countless horrors before their eyes. Those who were once loved now turned to violence and hunger, their bodies appearing to rapidly decay without seeming to die.


The first signs...

  • Irritability
    • People become irrationally angry at the slightest provocation - often yelling and becoming physically violent.
  • Increased Hunger
    • Those infected find themselves craving more and more food - specifically meat
  • Headache

Other signs...

  • Necrosis
  • Blindness
  • Loss of motor function
  • Loss of speech
  • Loss of hand-eye motor coordination
  • Sustained daze
  • Black spider-like rotting under the skin

Final stages...

  • Ragged breathing
  • Inability to communicate at all
  • Near total Necrosis of the body
  • Death
  • Post-mortem activity
    • Continued movement after death
    • Lingering instincts such as hunger and aggression
    • Walking
    • Attempts at vocalisation
